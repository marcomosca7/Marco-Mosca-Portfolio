HOME DEPOT

Home ownership is on the decline. It's hard for young professionals to justify buying the nice, expensive furniture they might want.

The Home Depot's DIY Summer Studio lets you build the furniture you want for cheaper, while adding your own personal touch — turning "someday" furniture into "I built this" furniture.

The Campaign
Phase 01
Out-of-Home

Transit benches and other outdoor placements are designed to trigger a single question: "Could I actually build this?" A scannable code takes people straight into DIY Summer Studio, where step-by-step guidance and Home Depot materials make the answer feel like a confident "yes."

Phase 02
The Buildability Plug-In

Fans of DIY Summer Studio can download the Buildability Plug-In for their browsers. The extension scans product pages and moodboards across the internet and flags anything that could be turned into a DIY project with The Home Depot — surfacing materials, tools, estimated time, and a direct link to open it as a project inside DIY Summer Studio.

Partnerships

Home Depot partnered with DIY content creators like Simone Giertz to get real reactions to our exclusive Summer DIY shed.

Microsite

QR codes lead to the DIY Summer Studio — a site that recognizes photos of furniture and provides instructions and a list of materials to build it, available for purchase at The Home Depot.

We didn't just sell materials — we sold the confidence that you could build something worth keeping.
